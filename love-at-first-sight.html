Love at First Sight (2025)

Poster & Brochure

This project is a two-sided 18" x 24" poster folded down to 9" x 12", built from a typographic exploration of Valentine de Saint-Point’s Futurist Manifesto of Lust. After researching the manifesto’s historical context and Saint-Point’s radical stance within Futurism, I isolated key lines and transformed them into a declarative typographic composition that reflects the text’s confrontational, sensual tone through sharp contrasts, counterforms, and dynamic spacing. This visual language then informed a contemporary event poster on the reverse side, featuring over 100 words of the manifesto set in structured text columns. When folded, the poster reveals two distinct but connected voices: expressive provocation on one side and clear event information on the other.
